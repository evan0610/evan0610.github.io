About Me

I am Evan Wu, an Electronic Engineering undergraduate in the ELITE Stream at The Chinese University of Hong Kong. My work focuses on medical robotics, robotic endoscopy, computer vision, and vision-language-action learning.

I am currently a Research Student Helper at the Robotics, Perception and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory at CUHK. My research involves developing multimodal robotic systems that integrate endoscopic video, motor states, anatomical and clinical labels, electromagnetic tracking, and real-time embedded control.

My current projects investigate autonomous colonoscopy withdrawal, supervised endoscopic navigation, multimodal action learning, and clinician-in-the-loop safety mechanisms. I am particularly interested in how embodied foundation models can improve the reliability, interpretability, and clinical usability of intelligent medical robots.

Selected Research Projects

ColonVLA
Motor-Based Mixture-of-Experts VLA for Autonomous Colonoscopy Withdrawal

Developing a motor-based mixture-of-experts vision-language-action framework for autonomous colonoscopy withdrawal on a six-actuator robotic endoscope platform.

  • Use latent action-mode decomposition to model inspection, tracking, sampling, recovery, and anatomy-dependent withdrawal control.
  • Integrate endoscopic video, robot motor states, clinical phase and anatomy labels, and electromagnetic tracker measurements into a synchronized learning pipeline.
  • Use three-dimensional tracker coordinates to estimate withdrawal velocity and support phantom-based validation.
EndoNav
Clinician-Supervised Autonomous Endoscopic Navigation

Designed a supervised autonomous navigation workflow for endoscopic intervention in which human instructions interrupt autonomous control and assign the operative instrument.

  • Separate clinician intervention from normal autonomous navigation to preserve clear operational control.
  • Use a binary verifier to determine whether autonomous execution should safely continue or terminate.
  • Evaluate the workflow through anatomical phantom experiments.
EndoVLA-OOV / OpenHDS

Collected and structured more than 3,000 endoscopic samples from anatomical phantoms for vision-language-action model development.

About Me

I am Tianchun Wu, an Electronic Engineering undergraduate in the ELITE Stream at The Chinese University of Hong Kong. My research focuses on vision-language-action learning, medical robotics, robotic endoscopy, multimodal perception, and embodied intelligence.

I develop and evaluate learning-based control systems for robotic endoscopy. My work includes reproducing and evaluating vision-language and vision-language-action baselines, fine-tuning multimodal foundation models on domain-specific endoscopic data, designing actuator-space action representations, and integrating trained models with physical robotic platforms.

I build end-to-end research pipelines covering synchronized video and robot-state acquisition, clinical phase and anatomy annotation, YOLO-assisted data labeling, vision-language model supervised fine-tuning, VLA policy training, offline evaluation, and anatomical phantom experiments.

On the robotics side, I work with STM32, CAN communication, ROS and ROS 2, six-motor endoscope control, electromagnetic tracking, real-time Python interfaces, and multimodal robot-state logging. My current work studies autonomous colonoscopy withdrawal, clinician-supervised navigation, and robust action generation under multimodal motor-space distributions.

Selected Research Projects

ColonVLA

Motor-Based Mixture-of-Experts VLA for Autonomous Colonoscopy Withdrawal

Developing a motor-based mixture-of-experts vision-language-action framework for autonomous colonoscopy withdrawal on a six-actuator robotic endoscope platform.

  • Formulate colonoscopy withdrawal as an actuator-space distribution decomposition problem rather than a generic direct action-regression task.
  • Use latent action-mode decomposition to model inspection, tracking, sampling, recovery, and anatomy-dependent withdrawal control.
  • Compare latent-gated experts, single-action experts, and GR00T-style continuous action baselines.
  • Integrate endoscopic video, motor states, clinical phase and anatomy labels, and electromagnetic tracker measurements into a synchronized multimodal training pipeline.
  • Use three-dimensional tracker coordinates to estimate withdrawal velocity and support phantom-based validation.

EndoNav

VLM-Guided Clinician-Supervised Autonomous Endoscopic Navigation

Engineering a clinician-supervised autonomous endoscopic navigation system that integrates a vision-language model, robot control policy, human-instruction interface, binary safety verifier, and multi-instrument execution workflow.

  • Process endoscopic observations, navigation history, task instructions, and robot states to generate autonomous control commands for forward navigation and target approach.
  • Reproduce and evaluate baseline vision-language models on instruction understanding, endoscopic scene interpretation, target recognition, and action-selection tasks.
  • Fine-tune domain-specific VLMs using anatomical phantom data, language instructions, visual observations, tool-selection labels, and control-state annotations.
  • Implement a control-state machine in which a human instruction immediately interrupts autonomous navigation and simultaneously specifies the instrument assigned to the operative channel.
  • Suspend navigation, update the active tool configuration, and transfer execution to the intervention workflow after receiving a clinician command.
  • Use a binary verifier that outputs 1 for valid continuation and 0 for termination.
  • Evaluate whether commanded motion produces sufficient physical progress, whether the robot remains within the permitted operating state, and whether autonomous control should continue.
  • Log synchronized endoscopic video, motor commands, actuator feedback, tool-selection signals, human instructions, verifier outputs, and robot states during anatomical phantom experiments.

About Me

I am Tianchun Wu, an Electronic Engineering undergraduate in the ELITE Stream at The Chinese University of Hong Kong.

My research interests lie at the intersection of medical robotics, robotic endoscopy, vision-language-action learning, computer vision, and embodied intelligence.

I am currently a Research Student Helper at the Robotics, Perception and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory at CUHK. My work involves multimodal robotic data collection, robotic endoscope control, embedded motor systems, electromagnetic tracking, visual perception, and vision-language-action model development.

My current projects focus on autonomous colonoscopy withdrawal, clinician-supervised endoscopic navigation, multimodal action learning, and safety-aware robotic autonomy.

Selected Research Projects

ColonVLA: Developing a motor-based mixture-of-experts vision-language-action framework for autonomous colonoscopy withdrawal on a six-actuator robotic endoscope platform.

The framework uses latent action-mode decomposition to model inspection, tracking, sampling, recovery, and anatomy-dependent withdrawal control.

Endoscopic video, motor states, clinical phase and anatomy labels, and electromagnetic tracker measurements are integrated into a synchronized multimodal learning pipeline.

EndoNav: Designed a clinician-supervised autonomous navigation workflow for endoscopic intervention.

Human instructions interrupt autonomous control and assign the operative instrument, while a binary verifier determines whether autonomous execution should continue or terminate.

EndoVLA-OOV / OpenHDS: Collected and structured more than 3,000 endoscopic samples from anatomical phantoms for vision-language-action model development.

Developed synchronized video, motor-state, annotation, and robot-state acquisition workflows.

Exploring Continuum Robot Agents for Fire Rescuing in Buildings : Developed a continuum-robot agent concept for rescue assistance in structurally constrained building-fire environments.

The project integrates robotic mobility, environmental perception, and task-level autonomy for navigation in constrained spaces.
